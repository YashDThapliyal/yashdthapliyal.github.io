Building Personal Superintelligence

The core of my work is answering a simple question: how do we build intelligent systems that understand context, maintain state, and act usefully in the real world?

Currently, I'm a researcher at Berkeley AI Research (BAIR), where I work on speech foundation models, multimodal learning, and world modeling. I interned at Amazon, where I built an agentic AI workflow automation system using AWS Bedrock and MCP to automate anomaly detection, root cause analysis, and operational triaging across 40,000+ support tickets, saving 25+ engineer hours per quarter. Most recently, I was at C.Light Technologies, where I built a hybrid reasoning layer that converts ambiguous natural-language queries into structured outputs for multi-agent routing and execution, applying language-model disambiguation and multi-turn context propagation to maintain conversational state.

I've been exploring multi-agent architectures, persistent memory, and multimodal reasoning, with a focus on making language and reinforcement learning systems capable of planning, adaptation, and feedback-driven improvement over time.

Long term, I want to build personal superintelligence: AI systems that deeply understand individual context, reason transparently, and compound human capability instead of replacing it.
